During a myocardial infarction, which of the following cardiac biomarkers is the most sensitive and specific for detecting initial myocardial cell injury within the first 3-6 hours?

info

Explanation

Cardiac Troponin I (cTnI) is considered the gold standard for diagnosing myocardial infarction. It is highly specific to cardiac muscle and begins to rise within 3 to 6 hours after the onset of chest pain.

While CK-MB was previously used, it is also present in skeletal muscle, making it less specific. Myoglobin rises earlier (1-3 hours) but lacks cardiac specificity. LDH is a late marker, rising 12-24 hours after injury.
